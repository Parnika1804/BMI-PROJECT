What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple calculation used to assess whether a person has a healthy body weight for a given height. It is calculated by dividing a person's weight in kilograms by the square of their height in meters.

Benefits of Knowing Your BMI

  • Helps identify if you're underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.
  • Can indicate potential health risks associated with being overweight or underweight.
  • Serves as a guideline for setting weight-related goals.
  • Useful for tracking changes in body weight over time.

How BMI Works

BMI is calculated using the following formula:

BMI = weight (kg) / (height (m))^2

This formula provides a numerical value that falls into categories:

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5 (Blue)
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5 - 24.9 (Green)
  • Overweight: BMI 25 - 29.9 (Orange)
  • Obesity: BMI 30 or higher (Red)
